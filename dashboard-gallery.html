Hospital Data Analysis & Insights — Tableau Dashboards

A multi-dashboard Tableau analysis of hospital Quality Data and Readmission Registry datasets, built to surface insights across provider specialties, length of stay, readmissions, ED throughput, mortality, ambulatory care, and blood pressure control — supporting data-driven improvements in patient care and operational efficiency.

Provider Specialty Overview

  • Funnel chart ranks provider specialties by headcount — Primary Care leads with 16 providers, followed by Pediatrics, Cardiology, and Surgery
  • Donut chart breaks down 945 total patient visits by specialty, with Primary Care (194) and Pediatrics (177) driving the highest volume
  • Packed bubble chart traces patient visits by year, highlighting 1993 and 1997 as peak years with 100 and 96 visits respectively

Length of Stay (LOS) Analysis

  • Treemap of primary diagnosis shows Pneumonia (177 days) and Heart Failure (78 days) as the largest contributors to total length of stay
  • 577 patients were discharged home versus 104 who expired and 39 transferred, segmenting LOS by discharge disposition
  • Average length of stay trended upward from 7.1 days in Week 2 to a peak of 14.0 days in Week 9, alongside rising discharge counts

Readmission Analysis Trend

  • Overall readmission KPI of 26.94%, with weekly rates ranging from a high of 45.92% in Week 1 to a low of 12.20% in Week 6
  • Stroke (76.47%) and Heart Failure (57.69%) post the highest readmission rates among primary diagnoses
  • Demographic breakdown highlights elevated readmission among Spanish-speaking and Black/African-American patient groups, informing targeted follow-up care

Emergency Department (ED) Throughput

  • Average ED throughput of 11.67 hours from patient arrival to discharge — a key driver of patient satisfaction
  • Throughput climbs with acuity level, peaking at 12.11 hours for the highest-acuity patients
  • Chest Pain (308 visits) and Shortness of Breath (149 visits) are the leading reasons for ED visits, each with throughput above the overall average

Mortality Analysis

  • Mortality index of 42.73%, comparing actual deaths against expected deaths within the observation period
  • Heart Failure and Flu account for the highest observed mortality by diagnosis
  • Mortality trended highest in January (94.45 expected vs. observed deaths), tapering by March, with demographic splits surfacing disparities by race and gender

Ambulatory Care Snapshot

  • 320 new visits out of 736 total completed, with an average no-show rate of 16.21% and average wait time of 15.60 days
  • No-show rate fluctuates sharply week to week, spiking to 40.63% before dropping to 0% — pointing to scheduling and outreach opportunities
  • Follow-up visits (266) outpace new (120), physical (155), and telemedicine (195) visit types in total completed volume

Blood Pressure (BP) Control Trend

  • Overall BP control KPI of 41.47%, tracked weekly against a target of under 120/80 mm Hg
  • Control rates vary widely by demographic group, from 45% to 100%, with Black/African-American female patients showing the strongest control
  • New patient visits (56%) show the highest share of BP readings, ahead of Follow-Up (40%), Physical (40%), and Telemedicine (38%) visit types

Approach

This analysis followed a three-phase framework: Tableau for initial data exploration, chart building, table joins, and date/null-value handling; Python (NumPy, Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn) for deeper exploratory analysis, pair plots, ICU length-of-stay and mortality investigation, and patient demographic stratification; and SQL for data validation, integrity checks, ERD-based database design, and automated triggers — confirming findings across all three tools before drawing conclusions.
